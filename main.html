About

My name is Mia Coupland and I am a junior software developer based in Newcastle Upon-Tyne. I am currently undertaking a Masters in Computer Science and have a background in history. I am a cultural historian who studied my undergraduate degree at the University of Manchester and I am interested in merging the two fields of history and computer science together. I would like to use development for humanitarian or accessibility purposes.

I have specialised in the history of China's one-child policy and the effects of international adoption, however I have always wanted to pursue software development and only recently received the opportunity. I am hoping to use my existing knowledge in this field to build my final dissertation project.

I have a wide range of experience from fraud prevention to IT and my current role is in respeaking/captioning for the deaf, hard of hearing, and foreign language speakers. As you can see, I love to learn new things and try an array of different experiences. Online accessibility is greatly important to me and I would like to carry over what I have learned from my current job into a future role, making the internet more accessible.
